Chapter I: Introduction
1.1 Background of the Study
In the digital age, Higher Education Institutions (HEIs) in the Philippines are increasingly pressured to modernize their administrative workflows. At the Don Mariano Marcos Memorial State University - South La Union Campus (DMMMSU-SLUC), the Computer Science department serves as a pioneer in technological adoption. However, a significant gap remains in the management of student community service records. Currently, the documentation of "outreach" or "extension" hours is often handled through manual logbooks or fragmented spreadsheet files. This manual approach is prone to "Information Asymmetry," where students, SBO officers, and faculty members have differing records of rendered service. The CSConnect Portal is proposed as a centralized, web-based solution designed to harmonize student information management with community service tracking, ensuring that every hour rendered is validated, stored, and reportable.
1.2 Statement of the Problem
The existing manual system for tracking community service at DMMMSU-SLUC faces several critical challenges: Data Redundancy: Repeated entry of student details across multiple event forms. Verification Delays: The time-lag between a student completing a service and the teacher/administrator verifying those hours. Lack of Accessibility: Students cannot check their total accumulated service hours in real-time, leading to confusion regarding graduation requirements.
1.3 Objectives of the Study
The primary goal is to develop the CSConnect Portal to achieve: Centralization: To integrate student profiles and community service histories into a single, secure MySQL database. Real-time Monitoring: To allow SBO officers to encode attendance digitally and for teachers to approve them instantly. Automated Reporting: To generate professional PDF or CSV reports for departmental and institutional audits.
Chapter II: Review of Related Literature and Sources
1.1 Management Information Systems (MIS) in Education According to Laudon & Laudon (2022), MIS provides the backbone for "Managing the Digital Firm." In an academic context, an MIS reduces "information silos" by ensuring that data from one department (like the SBO) is immediately available to another (the Faculty). This project applies these principles by creating a "Single Source of Truth" for student extension data. 1.2 Web-Based Tracking and User Acceptance The Technology Acceptance Model (TAM), first proposed by Davis (1989), suggests that the success of a system depends on "Perceived Usefulness." Al-Mamary et al. (2014) further validated that web-based systems in organizations significantly enhance decision-making quality. For CSConnect, the usefulness is defined by the student's ability to see their progress toward service milestones. 1.3 Data Integrity and Relational Databases The use of MySQL is supported by the work of Silberschatz, Korth, and Sudarshan (2019) in Database System Concepts. They argue that ACID (Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability) compliance is non-negotiable for academic records to prevent data corruption during simultaneous user logins. Chapter III: Methodology 1.1 The Agile Development Model The study adopts the Agile SDLC. Unlike the traditional Waterfall method, Agile allows the developer to build the system in "Sprints." Planning: Defining the requirements of DMMMSU-SLUC students and faculty. Sprint 1 (Backend): Setting up the Node.js environment and MySQL schema. Sprint 2 (Frontend): Developing the dashboard using HTML/CSS/JS. Testing: Using "Unit Testing" for login modules and "User Acceptance Testing" (UAT) with SBO officers. 1.2 Technical Stack Specifications Frontend: HTML5 for structure, CSS3 for the DMMMSU-themed styling, and Vanilla JavaScript for DOM manipulation. Backend: Node.js with Express.js. This choice was made because Node.js handles asynchronous operations efficiently, which is ideal for real-time attendance tracking. Database: MySQL hosted on a local or cloud server to manage relational data efficiently. 1.3 User Roles and Access Control Role Permissions Students View personal profiles, track earned service hours, and browse upcoming community projects. Teachers High-level administration, approving service hours, and generating departmental reports. SBO Officers System administration, encoding event details, and initial verification of student attendance. IV. System Architecture The CSConnect Portal follows a Client-Server Model. The client (browser) sends requests to the Node.js server, which interacts with the MySQL database to fetch or update records. This architecture ensures that sensitive student data is processed on the server side, minimizing security vulnerabilities. Chapter IV: System Architecture and Design 1.1 Functional Decomposition The system is divided into three primary modules based on user roles: Student Module: Profile viewing, service history log, and "Current Progress" tracker. SBO Officer Module: Event creation, QR or manual attendance encoding, and preliminary log submission. Teacher/Admin Module: Final verification/approval of logs, user management (adding/removing students), and high-level report generation. 1.2 Security Protocol To ensure compliance with the Philippine Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173), the system implements: Bcrypt Hashing: No plain-text passwords will be stored in the database. Session-based Authentication: Using Express-session to maintain secure user states. V.
